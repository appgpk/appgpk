# 👋 Introducing Myself

Hello! I'm **Marie Carmel**, a PhD student in Economics.

As an economist, I wear many hats. My work involves analyzing data, collecting and cleaning datasets, building theoretical models (which can often be tested empirically), designing experiments to test economic theories, and developing data pipelines that connect data collection tools with analysis environments.

Many of these activities are described in academic terms, but they closely correspond to roles commonly found in industry.

In industry terms, I work on:

- **Data analysis** – analyzing large datasets to uncover patterns and insights, with a strong emphasis on causal inference and empirical methods.
- **Data collection & data cleaning** – gathering and preparing data for analysis.
- **Statistical modeling & causal inference** – estimating relationships and testing hypotheses.
- **Experiment design** – designing and analyzing experiments (similar to A/B testing).
- **Data pipelines** – building workflows that move data from collection tools to analysis systems.
- **Data visualization & communication** – presenting results in a clear and actionable way.

Another hat I wear is **business owner**. I run a small business that sells shoes and accessories at affordable prices in **Burkina Faso**. As a small business owner, I manage tasks such as inventory and restocking, product pricing, promotion and marketing, and overall business operations.

Through this experience, I have developed a strong interest in improving **data availability and digital tools in African markets**. One approach I explore is **digitalization**, which I am currently implementing in my own business. Working at this intersection has allowed me to identify real-world frictions in data collection and management and to think critically about solutions that bridge traditional practices and digital technologies.
